⏱ Trade Frequency Analysis — Why ~0.4 Trades/Day?

Why only ~0.4 trades/day? The strategy is deliberately selective — it only fires when a high-quality setup aligns across regime classifier, ATR conditions, RSI, EMA stack, AND a minimum quality gate (58/100). On the ~38% of days classified as MEAN_REVERT (ranging market), most strategies are now blocked (BM/BR removed — they had 16–27% WR). Wednesday trades are also blocked to avoid pre-expiry theta decay.

Could we get to 1 trade/day? Yes, but at a cost. Lowering the quality gate from 58 → 45 would add ~30% more trades, but historical analysis shows quality 45–58 setups have only 38–42% win rate vs 58–70% for high-quality entries. More trades would reduce the Sharpe ratio. The current approach — fewer, higher-conviction trades with avg win/loss ratio of 3.5:1 — is mathematically superior to high-frequency lower-quality entries.

⚠️ Friday trades have 84% WR vs Wednesday 29% WR — the bot correctly avoids bad days. Forcing daily trades would drag performance down to those mid-week levels.

🚨 NEW FINDING — Thursday Expiry Risk: Thursday (Nifty weekly options expiry) shows 36% WR in 2Y and drops to 22% WR (NEGATIVE P&L) in the recent 1Y. 47 Thursday trades earned only +₹43k vs 64 Friday trades earning +₹946k. A Thursday expiry filter (similar to the existing Wednesday block) is the next recommended optimization — estimated +15–20% Sharpe improvement.

📦 How Lot Sizing Works

Current lot size: 65 units per lot (simulated uniform).
Real Nifty history: 75 units (pre-2020), 50 units (2020-2024), 25 units (Jul 2024 onwards). We use 65 throughout for consistent cross-period comparison.

Sizing logic: Starts at 1 lot. As capital grows above ₹1.1L → 2 lots, ₹1.5L → 3 lots, etc. Quality gate also caps lots (low-quality signal = max 1-2 lots). After 3+ consecutive losses, sizes down automatically.

Max lots: Capped at 5 lots (₹5L notional exposure at ₹150 ATM premium). Each lot at ₹150 premium × 65 qty = ₹9,750 capital at risk per lot.
